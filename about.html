About Retinol.bio

Retinol.bio is an independent, research-based education site about retinol, retinal and tretinoin. Our goal is simple: explain vitamin A skincare in clear language, grounded in evidence – not marketing.

We focus on what retinoids can realistically do, how to start safely, and when it makes sense to talk to a dermatologist instead of self-experimenting.

Who operates this website?

Retinol.bio is operated by ronarn AS, an independent digital health and technology company based in Norway. We run a small portfolio of niche health and skincare websites with a focus on:

  • Evidence-based information
  • Simple, honest explanations
  • Clear distinction between education and product promotion

How Retinol.bio stays independent

Retinol.bio does not sell skincare products and is not owned or controlled by any beauty brand.

  • We do not sell sponsored posts or paid “features”.
  • We do not accept money to write positive reviews.
  • We clearly separate educational content (Retinol.bio) from product comparisons (Retynol.com).

All educational content is based on ingredient science, dermatology guidelines and published research where available. We always encourage readers to talk to their own doctor or dermatologist for personalised advice.

Relationship with Retynol.com

Retinol.bio is an education-first site. When you are ready to compare actual products, we send you to our separate comparison hub:

Retynol.com – Independent retinol product comparison

Retynol.com focuses on side-by-side comparisons of popular retinol serums and treatments, including price-per-ml, texture, strength and ingredient profiles.

Affiliate links and funding

Retinol.bio itself is an information site. Some links from Retinol.bio point to Retynol.com, which in turn uses affiliate links to retailers like Amazon.

If you end up buying a product through those links, ronarn AS may earn a small commission – at no extra cost to you. This helps support the time and research that goes into creating and maintaining our content.

Commissions do not change the educational content on Retinol.bio or the topics we choose to cover.
